# AI Rules

## Purpose

AI tools may assist drafting, outlining, editing, and proofreading. They must not reduce accuracy, originality, or public-safety standards.

## Allowed uses

- brainstorming outlines
- improving clarity
- summarizing already safe source material
- generating neutral example structures
- proofreading for grammar and readability

## Non-negotiable rules

- Never provide confidential information to an AI tool.
- Never paste internal documents, source code, screenshots, or logs from employers.
- Never publish AI-generated content without human review.
- Never invent facts, metrics, incidents, or references.
- Never let AI output become a substitute for technical judgment.

## Content rules for AI-assisted writing

- Generalize every example.
- Rewrite generic AI phrasing into a more specific and useful explanation.
- Verify technical claims against official or primary sources when possible.
- Remove filler, repetition, and shallow advice.
- Ensure the final result sounds like professional engineering documentation.

## Attribution expectation

Formal attribution is not required for routine AI editing, but the human editor is fully responsible for:

- accuracy
- confidentiality
- originality
- public suitability

## Red flags

Do not publish content that:

- sounds generic but says little
- references tools or practices incorrectly
- contains fabricated examples
- includes private context
- overstates certainty without evidence
